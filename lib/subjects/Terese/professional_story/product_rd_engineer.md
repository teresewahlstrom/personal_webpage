**Terese builds with broad, multidisciplinary depth across the entire product lifecycle. She connects engineering decisions with manufacturability, user needs, operational realities, and long-term field support.**

The hardest product decisions are rarely isolated technical choices. Product work often becomes divided across silos-focusing strictly on CAD geometry, software functionality, or manufacturing setups. Her perspective is shaped by having worked actively across all of them, blending a rigorous **M.Sc. in Mechanical Engineering with Industrial Design from Lund University** with hands-on experience in physical carbon-fibre manufacturing, digital workflow application development in Flutter, and direct customer-facing technical delivery. This breadth helps her see how decisions made during design propagate into manufacturing complexity, support burden, operational constraints, and long-term product viability. Whether the product is a physical component, an industrial technology, or a digital tool, she looks beyond the isolated feature to ensure the wider system functions reliably under real-world conditions.

## Product R&D in practice

* **Product-system improvement through operational insight - Markforged:** Through customer training and direct production exposure, she identified recurring gaps between how the product ecosystem was initially configured and what customers needed for reliable long-term operation. These observations revealed where auxiliary equipment, workflow assumptions, and support requirements had not been sufficiently considered at the point of sale. She used that insight to improve sales guidance, configuration checklists, auxiliary-equipment recommendations, onboarding logic, and product-management prioritisation, helping teams make better product-system decisions and reduce avoidable friction for customers.
* **Surface-pattern design enablement - Volvo Cars / Polestar 2:** She collaborated with industrial designers to map complex interior-pattern concepts across double-curved surfaces for the Polestar 2. By developing a faster geometry-processing workflow, she reduced lead times from weeks to hours during a period of limited internal capacity, helping designers iterate more rapidly.
* **Physical product development - Formula Student:** She owned driver ergonomics and steering-wheel development while contributing to the carbon-fibre manufacturing group. For the steering wheel specifically, she translated tactile driver feedback into validated physical geometry through iterative clay prototyping, 3D scanning, CAD, and structural optimisation using Altair OptiStruct. She then manufactured the final design using carbon-fibre vacuum infusion and polyurethane grip moulding. The resulting vehicle placed 4th out of 113 combustion-class cars at Silverstone.
* **Digital workflow prototype - Independent development:** Prototyped a Flutter-based workflow application for representing complex operational flows as interactive, traceable flowcharts. The concept explores how visual process maps can move beyond static documentation and provide a clearer structure for navigating dependent tasks, branching logic, and human decision points.
