**Terese manages the chaotic realities of execution at the interfaces of a business. Her focus is on the gap between what functions believe has been agreed, what customers expect, what engineering can support, and what operations can actually deliver.**

Engineering drawings, commercial contracts, and factory schedules speak different languages, and misalignment between them quickly leads to broken commitments. She works in that friction zone, translating constraints between commercial, technical, and operational perspectives before commitments drift too far from what can actually be delivered. Her experience across R&D development, customer delivery, and factory operations allows her to make difficult trade-offs explicit so teams can align around a shared direction. Her approach does not rely on top-down authority, but on structured investigation and real-time coordination-helping cross-functional delivery hold up under real-world pressure.

## Cross-functional leadership in practice

* **Technical-commercial delivery - GE Additive:** She coordinated materials R&D, operators, suppliers, and sales around customer benchmarks involving unreleased material parameters and a demanding delivery schedule. When the standard route could not meet the requirements, she persuaded materials R&D to establish workable parameters, identified and secured an accelerated alternative HIP route, and aligned operators, suppliers, and shipments around the commercial milestone. This enabled the benchmarks to proceed despite technical and logistical uncertainty.
* **Cross-functional enablement - Markforged:** As Head of Training, she coordinated materials R&D, machine design, software, customer-facing teams, and commercial stakeholders to establish a shared understanding of how technology should be taught and supported. She designed and launched a global training programme that brought together business leaders, PhD-level specialists, and operators around a shared operational baseline. As operators gained confidence, she gradually handed over selected teaching responsibilities, strengthening internal training capacity and reducing dependence on a small number of experts.
