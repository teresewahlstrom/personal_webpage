**Terese treats organisational capability like an engineering problem: something that can be mapped, structured, tested, and improved. She builds the structures that let technical teams operate more independently and consistently.**

When a technical organisation encounters recurring friction-repeated questions, inconsistent decisions, unclear handoffs, troubleshooting dead ends, or manual workarounds-she treats those patterns as design signals. They often indicate that important workflows, decision logic, or expertise remain fragmented, implicit, or poorly connected. Her work focuses on making those underlying structures visible, then rebuilding them into frameworks, templates, workflows, diagnostic systems, and enablement mechanisms that reduce execution friction and help teams operate with greater clarity, consistency, and independence.

Her approach follows a consistent pattern: **Map** - **Structure** - **Enable**

* **Map:** Identify the knowledge, dependencies, and decision points that govern the system.
* **Structure:** Organise that logic into a form that others can understand and navigate.
* **Enable:** Build practical systems that help teams apply, reuse, and improve knowledge over time.

## Capability architecture in practice

* **Shared diagnostic capability - Markforged:** Seeing that failed parts carried valuable knowledge that was often discarded, she took the initiative to establish a physical defect-reference table as a shared organisational resource. She collected scrap builds, organised them by defect morphology and likely process stage, and connected the physical samples with the stories and observations behind them. Over time, operators and engineers began contributing additional defects themselves. The table became a shared reference point for operators, application engineers, field service, software and mechanical engineers, commercial teams, leadership, customers, and visiting prospects seeking to compare observations, debate root causes, explain process limitations, and build a stronger shared understanding of the technology.
* **Decision frameworks - Volvo Cars, GE Additive, and Markforged:** Seeing that important decisions often depended on fragmented specialist reasoning, she built structured Excel-based models, templates, and workflow frameworks that made the governing logic more explicit and reusable. These captured sourcing trade-offs, configuration logic, production-cycle behaviour, equipment constraints, consumable demand, application requirements, and recurring support decisions. This enabled sales, support, and engineering teams, as well as customers, to evaluate options more independently, reducing reliance on scarce domain experts.
* **Technical enablement architecture - Markforged:** She replaced a fragmented customer-training setup with a structured global enablement system. Previously, onboarding was not consistently aligned with Machine R&D and Materials R&D best practices, important knowledge remained scattered across experts, and specialists were repeatedly drawn into avoidable customer support. She brought the relevant expertise together, created a deliberate training architecture, authored about 30 reusable guides, and established clearer boundaries between first-line support and expert escalation. The resulting system supported over 100 participants globally, improved consistency for customers, strengthened operator capability, and reduced unnecessary dependence on scarce specialists.
