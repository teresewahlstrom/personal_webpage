# How well does this version of the chapter go along the others

It goes along the others **better than the previous R&D versions**, but it is now noticeably more direct and less “architected” than some peer chapters. I think that is mostly good.

The portfolio needs this chapter to do something different. Capability Architect is about making expertise scalable, Cross-Functional Leader is about organizational interfaces, Computational Engineer is about codifying logic, AI Systems is about trustworthy AI infrastructure, and Metal AM is specialist industrial depth. The Product R&D chapter should be the broad product-development bridge. Your current version does that.

Where it fits well:

| Dimension             | Fit with peer chapters                                                     |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Tone**              | Good. Direct, practical, less abstract.                                    |
| **Structure**         | Good. Same “in practice” logic, but without forcing an approach framework. |
| **Distinct role**     | Stronger now. It owns product definition + product building.               |
| **Overlap control**   | Better. AM, AI, computational work are present but not overexplained.      |
| **Reader usefulness** | Stronger. A non-specialist reader can understand how you help.             |

The strongest part is the new intro. It clearly positions this chapter as the one about **product definition and product realization**, across product forms. That makes it distinct from the peer chapters.

The one place where it is weaker than the others is the second paragraph:

```markdown
Her foundation is in Mechanical Engineering with Industrial Design. It gives her a broad engineering base across product development, user needs, interfaces, CAD, manufacturing, engineering logic, and digital systems.
```

Your own comment is right: **“digital systems” feels unearned there**, and education alone does not explain the physical + digital bridge. The peer chapters are stronger when they explain capability through repeated work patterns, not just background. For example, the Computational Engineer chapter connects logic to repeated problems and reusable models, not only education; the old R&D source also framed your range as education plus later programming, automation, computational geometry, and digital product concepts. 

I would replace that paragraph with this:

```markdown
Her foundation combines Mechanical Engineering with Industrial Design, industrial R&D experience, application engineering, customer-facing validation, production exposure, and independent digital development. This gives her a broad product-development base across user needs, interfaces, CAD, manufacturing, engineering logic, product behavior, and implementation reality.
```

That fits the peer chapters better because it says your range comes from **foundation + experience**, not education alone.

I would also adjust the second bullet slightly. Your instinct is right: the sentence adds value, but it should name product behavior more clearly. Suggested replacement:

```markdown
* **Translating user reality into product improvements – Markforged and GE Additive:** Many product problems appear where design intent, product behavior, configuration, documentation, sales, support, and real use do not fully align. Terese has worked close enough to users, production environments, customer questions, and operational workarounds to identify those gaps and turn them into product improvements.

  This included improvements to physical and software-enabled product behavior, revised sales guidance, clearer equipment and consumables recommendations, onboarding improvements, configuration guidance, workflow design, customer enablement, and product-management discussions about how the broader offering should support customer success.
```

And yes: change the third bullet title. Since the whole chapter is already about direct and indirect contribution, the third bullet should not carry that distinction alone.

Use:

```markdown
* **Building across product forms:** Terese builds physical artifacts, digital tools, product logic, service structures, workflows, and reusable decision support.
```

With those three changes, the chapter sits very well beside the others. It becomes the broad, practical Product R&D chapter: less philosophical than Capability Architect, less organizational than Cross-Functional Leader, less specialist than Metal AM, less technical-tool focused than Computational Engineer, and less AI-specific than AI Systems.
